📊 Hotel Revenue Analysis Project

This project focuses on analyzing hotel data to better understand customer behavior and measure the impact of cancellations on overall revenue and profitability.

The workflow started by using SSIS as an ETL tool to extract data from a CSV file and load it into SQL Server. Then, a data warehouse structure was built using Fact and Dimension tables, followed by designing a Star Schema and creating calculated measures. Finally, interactive dashboards and reports were developed using Power BI.

📈 Key Insights
Revenue Performance

The total revenue reached 11.34M, with 7.04M confirmed revenue and 4.29M lost due to cancellations.
This clearly shows that cancellations have a significant negative impact on the hotel’s profitability.

Cancellation Analysis

The cancellation rate is relatively high at 32.79%, with 11.8K canceled bookings out of 36.2K total bookings.
This indicates potential issues in booking policies or customer behavior patterns.

Market Segment Analysis

Most bookings come from online channels (14.7K bookings), followed by offline channels (7.4K bookings).
Online channels are the primary source of bookings and should be continuously optimized.

Lead Time Impact

The highest cancellation rates occur when the lead time exceeds 60 days.
This leads to revenue loss and reduces the opportunity to rebook rooms.

💡 Recommendations
Improve cancellation policies by applying fees for late cancellations and reducing flexibility for long-term bookings.
Manage early bookings by offering non-refundable deals and sending booking confirmation reminders before arrival.
Enhance online channels by improving user experience and continuously analyzing customer behavior.
Reduce revenue loss by applying controlled overbooking strategies to offset expected cancellations.
📌 Conclusion

The hotel demonstrates strong demand; however, the high cancellation rate remains the main challenge.
Addressing this issue will significantly improve both revenue and overall profitability.
